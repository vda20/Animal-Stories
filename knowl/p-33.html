The housework in my family was meant to be roughly divided up into:
  • the outdoor chores — to be done by the men (my father, brother, the old Malay tukang kebun [gardener]);
  • the indoor chores — to be done by the women (my mother, two aunts, us four daughters, two female servants).
As a tomboy, I am useless at the girly tasks, so I undertook the men’s jobs: tending to the garden (including mowing thelawn), scrubbing out the drains, washing the car and the dog (plus one disastrous attempt at giving the cat a bath). Feeding the crocodiles and mucking out their pond thus became my duty, which I was happy to take on, because it was meditative.
