When he was nicely settled in, I’d uncross my legs, thus forcing his chin to lose its comfortable perch and rousing him from his nap. Then I’d cross my legs the other way, so the foot sticking out would now be facing the other direction. Boo Bear would shuffle round to that foot and rest his chin on it, but once he started to doze off, I’d uncross my legs, and start the process all over again.
