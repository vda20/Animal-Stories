They were a yearly winter joint project, shared among the farm mistress, the neighbour down the road, and a friend on the other side of the main road down to Tarbes in the Pyrénées. As the farm has the space, the pigs would be housed there, until they were ready for the dinner table. When the time came, the men would come and take them away to be slaughtered, then brought back for turning into sausages, ham and pâté, which will get shared out.
